# Grounded Serendipity (The Bridge Protocol)

Use this when you need novelty that is *structurally sound*, avoiding the randomness trap of standard serendipity.

## Phase 1: Sense-Making
1. **Call:** `graph_discover_grounded({ nodes: 3, intensity: 0.2 })`
2. **The Output:** The system provides random nodes but asks you to find a **Real Bridge** first.
3. **Your Job:** Don't invent yet. Find the shared substrate or functional analogy that *actually exists* between these concepts.
   - *Ask:* "If I transplanted the mechanism of Node A into the domain of Node B, what would it explain?"

## Phase 2: Chaos Perturbation
1. **Call:** `graph_discover_grounded_chaos({ bridge: "Your articulated bridge", source_nodes: [...] })`
2. **The Output:** The system injects noise *into your bridge statement*.
3. **Your Job:** Now rationalize the noise. How does the corruption extend your real insight into new territory?

## Why use this?
- **Standard Serendipity (ANI):** High Novelty, Low Coherence risk. Good for breaking blocks.
- **Grounded Serendipity:** High Novelty, High Coherence. Good for building lasting theory.
